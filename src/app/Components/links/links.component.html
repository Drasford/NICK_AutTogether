Autism spectrum disorder

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) refers to a range of conditions characterised by some degree of impaired social behaviour, communication and language, and a narrow range of interests and activities that are both unique to the individual and carried out repetitively. ASDs begin in childhood and tend to persist into adolescence and adulthood. In most cases the conditions are apparent during the first 5 years of life.

What is Autism ?

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), refers to a broad range of conditions characterized by challenges with social skills, repetitive behaviors, speech and nonverbal communication. According to the Centers for Disease Control, autism affects an estimated 1 in 54 children in the United States today.

Educating autistic students

With chronic problems like autism, health care providers all too often underestimate the commitment required by families to help their children become the best they can be. In this issue, two parents graciously share with us stories of how the whole family and the community help their children and what these special children need to grow. We are deeply grateful to these parents as their words tell the tale far more powerfully than any academic article. We hope that you, the reader, will share these pieces with parents, teachers, hockey and soccer coaches, and others in the community who help. With autism, it truly does take a village to raise a child.
